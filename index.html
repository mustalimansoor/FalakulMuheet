A Journey Through the Cosmos

From al-Falak al-Muheet, the Surrounding Orbit, to the earth upon which Maulana al-Imam al-Husain AS placed his blessed brow.

The Perfection of Creation

The universe and everything within it was created by Allah Taʿala through prior knowledge—not by trial and error. Every entity was placed precisely where it belongs. In the same manner, the tenets and principles of faith have been established: deliberately, perfectly, and without flaw.

The Nine Aflaak

The nine celestial orbits, from the all-encompassing al-Falak al-Muheet to the Moon, represent a divine hierarchy. Just as physical planets affect the temporal realm, the spiritual aflaak influence souls, guiding them toward salvation.

Celestial Alignments in History

500 Years Ago

The seat of Dawat transferred from Yemen to Hind. Syedna Yusuf Najmuddin RA, whose name contains ‘Najm’ (star), established 24 shops in Sidhpur, mirroring the 24 hours of al-Falak al-Muheet’s rotation.

100 Years Ago (1347 H)

Syedna Taher Saifuddin RA took the mithaq of Syedna Mohammed Burhanuddin RA in Surat, bestowing upon him the title Burhanuddin.

50 Years Ago (1397 H)

Syedna Mohammed Burhanuddin RA presided over ʿAshara Mubaraka in Madras, his twelfth as Dai Mutlaq.

This Year (1447 H)

This serendipitously marks Syedna Mufaddal Saifuddin's TUS twelfth ʿAshara Mubaraka as Dai Mutlaq. "Twelve plus twelve," he said, "is twenty-four."

Al-Falak al-Muheet

The outermost celestial sphere, representing the throne (ʿarsh) of Allah, manifests five key characteristics, each reflected in Wali Allah AS.

1. Rapid Revolution

It revolves on its own with great speed, the origin of all motion. Wali Allah is always the first to take action, guiding Mumineen by initiating it himself.

2. Perfectly Spherical

Its round shape symbolizes humility in bowing before the Almighty and the compassion of a loving parent bending towards their child.

3. Self-Sustaining Motion

Its rotation requires no external force. Similarly, Awliya Allah AS connect Mumineen to the center through muhabbat, ensuring they do not go astray.

4. Transparency (Shaffaafiyya)

It is subtle and immaterial. Nearness to Maula is not physical but spiritual, measured by the muhabbat and orientation of the soul.

5. Immense Vastness

Its vastness is beyond comprehension. In the same way, the embrace of Wali Allah AS is immeasurable, encompassing all Mumineen, no matter their condition.

The Land of Karbala

Ultimately, on ʿAshura, the focus is the Earth, upon which these celestial spheres exert their influence, and upon which Imam Husain AS, the divine star, descended to raise countless individuals to the heavens.

Imam Husain’s AS horse came to a halt. He mounted seven horses, yet none would move. He sighed deeply and asked for the name of the place. An elder replied that the exact spot was called ‘Karbala’! Hearing this, Imam AS exclaimed, “Karb (distress) and balaa (hardship)!”

He instructed Imam Ali Zain al-Abidin AS to convey his salaam to the faithful and to tell them that it behoved them to remember him whenever they partook of food or water.

May Allah Taʿala bless our beloved Maula with a long life in health and happiness until the Day of Qiyamat.
